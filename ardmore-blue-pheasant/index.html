Shopify Platform Evaluation

Ardmore Home Design + Blue Pheasant

A summary of why Shopify fits the portfolio, what we covered in the June 26 demo, and a clear breakdown of platform and payment costs — prepared for internal review with leadership.

Prepared for Kenar Maronian Following the June 26 demo Shopify Plus

Why Shopify

The goal is to grow the retail (DTC) channel and give the team direct control over how the brand is merchandised and told online — without a developer in the loop for every change. Shopify Plus supports that across the full Ardmore portfolio: Blue Pheasant, Made Goods, Pigeon & Poodle, and Burton James.

The Trigger

Own the storytelling

Today, custom category and product-page layouts in Magento require a third-party developer for nearly every change. Shopify's drag-and-drop editor lets the merchandising team build and adjust collection pages, PDPs, and editorial content directly — the entertaining-led, lifestyle layouts the brand is known for, managed in-house.

The Mandate

Grow the retail channel

With leadership now prioritizing DTC, the platform needs to move quickly and convert well. Shopify ships with conversion built in — Shop Pay accelerated checkout, fast hosting, and a storefront the team can iterate on continuously rather than in dev cycles.

One Platform

DTC and B2B together

Shopify Plus runs both the retail storefront and native B2B wholesale — customer-specific catalogs, tiered and volume pricing, gated login, net terms, and bulk ordering — on one platform. Retail comes first; wholesale can follow in a later phase without re-platforming.

No Disruption

Sage X3 stays the system of truth

Shopify integrates with the existing Sage X3 (v12) environment and middleware — product, customer, pricing, and inventory data continue to live in the ERP. Shopify is the storefront and commerce layer on top, not a replacement for the operational backbone.

Phased and low-risk. The aligned plan is retail (DTC) first, wholesale later. Shopify Plus includes dev and staging environments, so the team can build and test content and configurations safely before anything goes live.

Payments & Pricing

There are two separate costs, and they are not stacked the way the "double fee" question suggested. Here is exactly how each works.

Cost 1

Platform fee — the cost to be on Shopify Plus

This is the software/platform subscription. On a 3-year term it is the greater of a flat monthly minimum or a small variable rate on sales — you pay one or the other in a given month, never both. At typical retail volumes this lands at the monthly minimum the large majority of the time.

Included in the platform fee

What the subscription covers

  • Hosting and unlimited bandwidth
  • Built-in security and PCI compliance
  • Full native B2B toolset (customer accounts, tiered pricing, net terms)
  • Checkout extensibility and customization
  • Up to 9 expansion stores — useful across the Ardmore brand portfolio
  • 24/7 priority Plus support and a 99.99% uptime SLA
Cost 2

Payment processing — the cost to accept a card

This is completely separate from the platform fee, and it is a cost you already pay today through Stripe. With Shopify Payments, online card rates are:

Shopify Payments — online card rateRate
Domestic cards2.25% + 30¢
Premium cards2.95% + 30¢
International cards3.25% + 30¢

For B2B, ACH bank transfer is also available with tiered rates that fall as order value rises, capped at $7.50 per transaction.

On the "double fee" question. With Shopify Payments there is no extra transaction fee taken on top of card processing — you pay the platform fee and the processing rate, and that is it. The only time an additional fee applies is if you keep a third-party gateway (such as Stripe) instead of Shopify Payments, in which case Shopify adds 0.20% on top of the gateway's own rate. Using Shopify Payments removes that 0.20% entirely. The "double fee" scenario only exists with an outside gateway.

The right comparison

Card processing is not a new cost — it replaces what you pay Stripe today. The accurate comparison is the Shopify Payments rate against your current Stripe rate. Share your current rate and we will put the two side by side for leadership.
