Project Overview

Caravela is replacing its custom NetSuite-backed "Roaster Hub" with a self-service Shopify B2B portal. NetSuite stays the system of truth; Shopify becomes the customer-facing layer. Phase 1 is the spot coffee business with a sample-first flow — architected once, launched iteratively, targeting October 2026.

Who They Are

Green Coffee Trader — ~90% B2B, 7 Origins, 4 Continents

Caravela is a ~25-year-old green coffee trader sourcing from seven Latin American origins (Colombia largest) with offices across Houston, Dublin, London, and Australia. The business is ~90% B2B / 10% emerging DTC, managing roughly $5M in green coffee across 300+ accounts. The goal: move from manual, email-driven trading to a self-service platform that can serve thousands of North American micro-roasters.

B2B Spot Portal Sample-First Flow NetSuite (System of Truth) Atlas (Product/Tasting Data) Multi-Currency
Target Architecture
  • NetSuite remains the system of truth for inventory, pricing, and invoicing.
  • Shopify owns the customer-facing storefront, payments, the gated experience, and order releases.
  • Customer approval workflow: register in Shopify (lands "not approved") → syncs to NetSuite for review → NetSuite grants access back.
  • Contracting workflow: customer approves samples in the portal → triggers NetSuite to formalize the contract → NetSuite syncs a contracted price list back to Shopify.
Phase 1 Scope
  • Spot coffee only (~15% of inventory). Forward contracting (~85%) is Phase 2 — but architect once.
  • Sample-first path is primary; full-product ordering follows.
  • Samples: charging model + messaging to discourage over-ordering (no hard limits).
  • Personalized B2B catalogs by location/role; templated PDPs via metafields (harvest date, altitude, tasting notes).
  • Payment terms per account (CC / ACH / due-on-fulfillment); warehouse-release fulfillment.
Open Items to Resolve Before Build
  • Atlas integration path — connect product/tasting-note data via NetSuite or directly to Shopify (Caravela investigating).
  • Full-order invoicing — Shopify-native invoices vs. linked NetSuite PDFs.
  • Implementation partner — build internally vs. with a Shopify-experienced B2B + NetSuite partner (introductions underway).
  • Leadership sign-off — Caravela presenting the demo and direction to company leadership.

B2B Capabilities Demo Recap

June 18, 2026 — Mercedes Boykin-Sabalones & Jordan Murphy (Shopify), Nicole Freydell & Luz Gomez (Caravela). The proposed architecture meets ~90% of requirements; timeline is "as soon as possible."

Headline: Caravela confirmed the proposal meets roughly 90% of their requirements and wants to move as soon as possible. Next: bring in an implementation partner, build this mutual action plan, and present to leadership.

Decisions Aligned
  • Sample quantity strategy: charging model + user messaging to discourage excessive ordering — not hard limits.
  • Sample vs. purchase separation: sample ordering and full-product contracting are separate platform modules for a simpler UX.
  • System of record: NetSuite remains the source of truth for inventory, pricing, and invoicing; Shopify owns the customer interface and payment processing.
  • Launch approach: iterative — ship an initial release, then improve.
What We Demonstrated
  • Unified global admin — multi-geo, multi-currency, multi-language in one backend.
  • Unauthenticated experience + configurable account registration landing as "not approved" until NetSuite syncs approval.
  • Personalized B2B catalogs by location and role.
  • Configurable payment terms, warehouse-release fulfillment, one-off shipping addresses per cafe.
  • Shopify Flow automation, metafield-driven PDPs, Sidekick AI, and agentic-commerce visibility controls.
Open Questions Surfaced
  • Atlas integration path — via NetSuite or direct to Shopify (Caravela investigating).
  • Full-order invoicing — Shopify-native vs. linked NetSuite PDFs.
  • Build vs. partner — internal team vs. Shopify-recommended partner.
  • Commercial / paper process — not yet started; pending leadership sign-off.
