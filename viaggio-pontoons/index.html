Who They Are

Multi-Brand Manufacturer, Bristol Indiana — 137 Dealers Across the US

Viaggio Pontoons is the lead brand in a portfolio of four manufactured goods companies based in Bristol, Indiana — alongside Lakota Trailers, SMC, and Duo Form. Nathaniel Talbot, Director of IT and ERP Solutions Manager, brings 15 years of enterprise IT experience and a strategic vision that extends across all four brands: build the platform once for Viaggio, then extend it to Lakota, SMC, and Duo Form on shared middleware. NetSuite remains the system of truth for inventory and product data; Shopify Plus serves as the user experience layer — central dealer dashboard, configurator host, and D2C boat owner portal. The current SuiteCommerce front end was described by NetSuite's own engineers as not designed for B2B workflows, and incremental UI changes routinely take six or more months through third-party developers. The ERP itself remains in place. Target go-live: January 2027, with the 2027 boat show as the full brand marketing reveal.

Why They're Replacing the SuiteCommerce Front End
  • SuiteCommerce was not designed for B2B — confirmed by NetSuite's own engineers. The current front end does not align with Viaggio's dealer workflows.
  • Front-end changes routinely require six or more months through third-party developers, placing a sustained burden on Viaggio's single internal IT resource.
  • SuiteCommerce does not support a headless configurator, hybrid B2B/D2C contextualization, or flexible dealer lot inventory — capabilities required by Viaggio's project scope.
  • The multi-brand replication goal — extending the platform to Lakota, SMC, and Duo Form — is not achievable within SuiteCommerce's tightly coupled architecture. NetSuite ERP remains in place; only the commerce layer changes.
What They Need to Build
  • Dealer configurator — rule-based option logic with hundreds of dependent options, outputs bill of materials, used by customers + dealers + sales team in one tool
  • B2B dealer portal — 137 dealers, 1–15 users per dealer, Microsoft Entra SSO for internal staff, dealer-admin user management
  • Warranty management — replace the existing third-party NetSuite tool with a Shopify app partner (currently under evaluation)
  • D2C owner portal — boat owner warranty registration, model-year ownership docs, recall notices
  • Buyback / refurb workflow — draft/inactive flag so boats don't show on site until ready; treated as separate SKUs from new versions
  • Mobile app for sales reps — custom build on Shopify APIs
  • NetSuite integration — Phase 1 Oracle connector, long-term Celigo/Patchworks middleware for scaling to dealer DMS

Open Decision #1 — Configurator

Build vs. Buy

The configurator must support hundreds of options across pontoon size, tubes, rails and LED packages, seating, and electronics, with dependent rule logic between selections (e.g., RGB lighting requires the LED option; heated co-captain seats require the bolstered seat package). It outputs a bill of materials rather than discrete SKUs and supports three user roles in a single tool: customer configuration, dealer upsell, and final sales team review.

Decision criterion: if an off-the-shelf tool requires significant customization (~$200K+) to align with Viaggio's workflow, a custom build is preferred. Jordan will evaluate CPQ partner options — which may exceed budget — against a custom implementation on Shopify primitives (Metaobjects, Shopify Functions, Hydrogen).

Open Decision #2 — Warranty Management

App Partner vs. Custom

Viaggio's current third-party warranty package within NetSuite is operationally limited, requiring significant manual configuration. Replacing it with a Shopify-native warranty and claim management app partner is the preferred direction. Core requirements: product registration with dealer validation, second- and third-owner tracking, and a streamlined claim workflow.

Path forward: Mercedes and Jordan will present warranty and claim management app partners during the implementation partner evaluation phase.

Integration Path — NetSuite
  • Phase 1: Oracle's Shopify connector. Includes a license cost. Suitable for standard catalog and order synchronization at Viaggio's current pricing model complexity.
  • Long-term: Middleware (Celigo or Patchworks) for iPaaS. Required to scale once dealer DMS (dealer management system) integration enters scope. Nathaniel is Celigo-certified, which provides Viaggio with internal expertise on this path.
  • Rationale for phased approach: Viaggio's current pricing model is straightforward (MSRP-based percentages with volume tiers, no dealer-specific price lists), which the Oracle connector handles natively. Edge cases and future growth requirements will inform the middleware decision in a later phase.
Payments — Current & Future

Current State

  • ACH transfers and standard invoicing are handled entirely in NetSuite
  • No credit card processor is currently configured
  • Pricing model: MSRP-based percentages with volume tier discounts (e.g., a four-boat order unlocks pricing above a two-boat order). No dealer-specific price lists at present.
  • Given Viaggio's price points (~$150K per boat), dealer-specific pricing introduces cross-dealer competition risk and is not a near-term requirement

Future Opportunity

  • Aftermarket parts and accessories: credit card processing becomes relevant when D2C aftermarket commerce enters scope
  • Shopify Payments: $0.50 maximum fee per payment transaction — a competitive option relative to standard processor rates
  • Opens the option to process invoice payments through Shopify when Viaggio elects to do so
  • Not a Phase 1 dependency; captured for the future expansion roadmap
Other Confirmed Scope Items
  • Dealer portal: 1–15 users per dealer. Chain dealers typically operate one billing location with three to four sales and service locations. Dealer administrators manage their own user lists.
  • Single sign-on: Microsoft Azure Entra confirmed for internal employees. Dealer SSO mechanism to be determined. Jordan to validate the Entra and Shopify SSO pattern for external users.
  • Buyback and used boat resale: Boats are repurchased from dealers or customers, refurbished as needed, and resold as used inventory. The platform must support a draft/inactive flag so refurbished units do not appear on the site until ready. Returned boats are treated as separate SKUs from their new equivalents.
  • Sales rep mobile application: Custom build on Shopify APIs. No requirements beyond those documented in the RFI.
  • Knowledge base: The current Dropbox-linked knowledge base contains a significant volume of outdated documentation. Nathaniel will identify current source material; legacy content will be retired during migration.

Shopify Delivers

Eight requirements from Viaggio's RFI where Shopify Plus meets or exceeds expectations — natively or with a clear, well-scoped build pattern.

  • 1
    Section 9.2 · B2B Dealer Portal
    137 Dealers, Multi-Tenant Isolation — Native
    Each dealer is a Company in Shopify B2B — with their own Company Location, Contacts, price list, and payment terms. Data isolation between dealers is enforced at the platform level. A dealer logged in cannot see another dealer's orders, inventory, or registration data. Shopify Plus B2B Company Accounts are designed for exactly this architecture. No custom multi-tenancy code required.
  • 2
    Section 9.1 · Dealer Configurator
    Rule-Based Configurator on Hydrogen + Shopify Infrastructure
    Shopify doesn't sell a boat configurator. What it provides is the platform to build one correctly: Storefront API for commerce data, Shopify Functions for business logic evaluation, Metaobjects as the structured rule store (editable by Viaggio admins without a code deployment), and Hydrogen as the React framework. The configurator lives as a headless application on Vercel — independent from the storefront, embeddable anywhere, and not constrained by Shopify's checkout flow. This is the architecture that meets every configurator requirement in the RFI.
  • 3
    Section 9.1.4 · Dual Pricing
    MSRP / Dealer Cost Dual Pricing — Native, No Custom Code
    An unauthenticated visitor to the public configurator sees MSRP. A dealer who logs into their B2B portal session sees their dealer cost. Shopify B2B price lists respond to the authenticated session context automatically — the same configurator serves both audiences. This is a core feature of Shopify B2B, not a workaround.
  • 4
    Section 9.1.5 · WordPress Embed
    Configurator Embeds in WordPress — WordPress Stays Unchanged
    Because the configurator is a headless application on Vercel, it is completely independent of Shopify's storefront layer. It can be embedded in any web context — including the current WordPress site — via iframe or a lightweight JavaScript initialization script that passes model ID, user role, and authentication context. WordPress does not need to be migrated or rebuilt. This is an architectural property of headless, not a feature request.
  • 5
    Section 9.3 · NetSuite Integration
    Phased Integration — Oracle Connector Now, Middleware Long-Term
    Phase 1: Oracle's Shopify connector handles standard catalog and order synchronization. Viaggio's current pricing model (MSRP-based percentages with volume tiers, no dealer-specific price lists) is well within the connector's native capability. Long-term: middleware — Celigo or Patchworks — for full iPaaS coverage once dealer DMS integration enters scope. Nathaniel holds a Celigo certification from a prior role, which provides Viaggio with in-house expertise on the middleware path. NetSuite remains the system of truth for all inventory and product data; Shopify operates as the user experience layer. The integration pattern is standard, the stack is well-supported, and no Shopify constraint blocks any of the integration flows described in the RFI.
  • 6
    Section 9.2.4 · Lot Inventory
    Hull Records as Metaobjects — Not a Product Catalog
    Hull IDs are not products. They're records — with a hull ID, model, year, options, status, dealer assignment, and lot entry date. Shopify Metaobjects model this precisely: Admin API creates a hull record when NetSuite fires a "boat shipped" webhook, updates it when the registration is submitted, and marks it "sold" when a retail registration completes. Dealers see their live lot via the portal. Nothing gets forced into a product catalog paradigm it doesn't fit. The architecture is purpose-built for Viaggio's lot management workflow.
  • 7
    Section 9.4 · Mobile PWA
    Installable PWA — No App Store, Camera Access, Offline Queue
    A Hydrogen + Next.js application on Vercel supports full PWA capability: installable to home screen on iOS and Android with no App Store submission, camera access via the browser MediaDevices API (bill of sale photograph, hull ID QR scan, delivery photos), and an offline service worker + IndexedDB queue for registration submissions at boat shows or dealer lots with poor connectivity. These are standard web platform capabilities — not Shopify-specific features — and they work correctly in the recommended headless stack.
  • 8
    Payments · ACH Today, Optional Shopify Payments Future
    Keep Current ACH + Invoicing Flow. Door Open for Aftermarket Parts via Shopify Payments.
    Current state: ACH transfers and standard invoicing flow through NetSuite. No credit card processor is configured, which is appropriate for Phase 1. Shopify B2B net-terms ordering bypasses payment gateways entirely, meaning dealer orders incur no transaction fee regardless of which payment method Viaggio uses on the back end. Future state: when aftermarket parts and accessories enter scope on the D2C side, Shopify Payments enables credit card processing with a $0.50 maximum fee per payment transaction — a competitive option given Viaggio's price points. Not a Phase 1 dependency; captured for the future expansion roadmap.
  • 9
    Nathaniel's Explicit Requirement
    No IP Lock-In. Current Tech Standards. Architecture Replicates to All Four Brands.
    Everything Shopify stores is accessible via Admin API — products, orders, customers, Metaobjects, company accounts — exportable at any time in standard formats. The configurator is a React/Next.js application, no proprietary framework. Rules are stored as JSON in Metaobjects. If Viaggio migrates off Shopify, they keep the configurator and the rules. For multi-brand replication: one Shopify Organization, expansion stores per brand (Viaggio → Lakota → SMC → Duo Form), shared middleware. The architecture Nathaniel designs for Viaggio is, by default, the architecture he extends to the other three brands without rebuilding anything.

What requires custom development: The configurator UI, multi-step delivery registration workflow, lot inventory interface, e-signature integration, two-phase registration state machine, offline service worker, and push notification layer are all custom builds on top of Shopify's platform infrastructure. This is expected for a project of this scope. The platform provides the commerce layer, data layer, and API surface — the applications that run on top are purpose-built for Viaggio.
